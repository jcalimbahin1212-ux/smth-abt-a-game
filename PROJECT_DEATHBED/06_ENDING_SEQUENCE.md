# PROJECT DEATHBED — ENDING SEQUENCE

## THE FINAL CONFRONTATION

*"My story, my history / I held the hope of being free..."*

---

## CONTEXT

This is the climactic scene of PROJECT DEATHBED. Everything has led here: the caretaking, the memory anchors, the relationships, the understanding that the Light is lonely but lethal, and the brothers' unbreakable bond.

The player has made choices throughout the game that affect specific details, but the emotional core remains constant: Adrian sings to Luis as the Light takes them both, refusing to let his brother be overwritten, refusing to let him die alone.

## PHASE 1: THE THRESHOLD

**Setting:** The abandoned community center, dawn. Light activity has been building all night. The fractures above are more active than ever.

**Gameplay:** The player has one final opportunity to reinforce Luis's most important memory anchors.

---

*Morning. Luis sits in a patch of filtered light. His skin is nearly translucent. His veins glow steadily. The hum is constant.*

*Adrian approaches with water, medication—the routine.*

**ADRIAN**
Morning.

*Luis turns. His eyes are bright—too bright. But he smiles.*

**LUIS**
(clear)
Hey.

**ADRIAN**
(relieved)
You're still here.

**LUIS**
For now.

*The routine begins. Temperature check—critical. Lucidity—present but fragile. Shape integrity—low.*

**ADRIAN**
How are you feeling?

**LUIS**
Warm. Always warm now.

*He looks at his hands. The glow pulses with his heartbeat.*

**LUIS**
It's coming. The Light. It's almost ready.

**ADRIAN**
Ready for what?

**LUIS**
To finish learning me. To... translate.

*The hum rises, slightly.*

**LUIS**
(looking up)
I can feel it waiting.

*[PLAYER CHOICE: Adrian can ask Luis about specific memories—one final reinforcement session.]*

---

**Memory Reinforcement Options:**

If player chooses "Mom's Song":

**ADRIAN**
Tell me about Mom's song. The one she sang to us.

**LUIS**
(closing eyes)
She'd come into our room. Sit between our beds. Sing until we fell asleep.

**ADRIAN**
What do you remember of the words?

**LUIS**
"My story, my history..."

*He trails off. Adrian picks up.*

**ADRIAN**
"I held the hope of being free."

**LUIS**
(grasping it)
"The love that would hold me... I promised care for thee..."

**ADRIAN**
(voice breaking)
"I've told you I'm sorry. I was just too careless."

**LUIS**
(opening eyes)
That's us. That's our song.

---

If player chooses "Who You Are":

**ADRIAN**
Tell me who you are.

**LUIS**
(struggling)
I'm... Luis. Luis Navarro.

**ADRIAN**
What else?

**LUIS**
I taught music. I loved it. I loved the kids.

*He pauses, searching.*

**LUIS**
I have a brother. Adrian. He takes care of me.

**ADRIAN**
What else about him?

**LUIS**
(smiling faintly)
He's stubborn. He worries too much. He makes terrible coffee.

**ADRIAN**
(laughing softly)
It's not that bad.

**LUIS**
It's really bad.

*The memory holds. Luis is still himself.*

---

**[The reinforcement concludes. Luis steadies, temporarily.]**

*Mae appears in the doorway.*

**MAE**
Adrian. You need to see this.

*They move to a window. Outside, the sky is shifting. Fractures are converging.*

**MAE**
It's never done this before. They're... gathering.

**ADRIAN**
(quiet)
It's coming for him.

**MAE**
(no argument)
What do you want to do?

**ADRIAN**
What I've always done. Stay with him.

*Mae nods. She understands.*

**MAE**
The convoy will move in an hour. If you're not with us...

**ADRIAN**
I know.

*She holds his gaze for a moment. Then turns away.*

**MAE**
(at the door)
Thank you. For showing us what it looks like. Caring for someone until the end.

*She's gone.*

---

## PHASE 2: THE TRANSLATION BEGINS

**Setting:** The same room. The Light begins to descend. Luis enters the final stage.

**Gameplay:** Limited. The player watches more than acts now.

---

*Adrian returns to Luis. The hum is louder now—not just sound, but pressure.*

**LUIS**
(trembling)
It's starting.

*The walls seem to brighten. Not from outside light—from the Light pressing in.*

**ADRIAN**
I'm here.

*He sits beside Luis. Takes his hand.*

*Luis's eyes flare brighter. His body stiffens. He gasps.*

**LUIS**
(layered voice—his own and something else)
It's... inside. It's reading me.

**ADRIAN**
Stay with me, Luis. Focus on my voice.

*Luis's head turns slowly, unnaturally.*

**LUIS / THE LIGHT**
*Shape. Pattern. You are... song. You are... repeating.*

**ADRIAN**
(steady)
That's right. I'm repeating. I'm not leaving.

*Luis shudders. For a moment, he's himself again.*

**LUIS**
(desperate)
Adrian, it wants everything. It wants to know all of me.

**ADRIAN**
Then we'll give it what it wants. On our terms.

*He pulls Luis close. Holds him.*

**ADRIAN**
I'm going to sing now. Okay?

**LUIS**
(crying)
Okay.

---

## PHASE 3: THE SONG

**Setting:** The room is now filled with light. The brothers at the center.

**Gameplay:** The player controls the singing—selecting lines, maintaining rhythm through a gentle mechanic that mirrors breathing.

---

*Adrian begins to sing. His voice shakes, but he doesn't stop.*

**ADRIAN** (singing)
*"My story, my history..."*

*The Light pulses. It's listening.*

**ADRIAN** (singing)
*"I held the hope of being free..."*

*Luis's eyes focus on Adrian. Tears stream down his glowing face.*

**[GAMEPLAY: The player selects the next line. If memory anchors were strong, the correct option is highlighted. If not, the player must remember from earlier scenes.]**

**ADRIAN** (singing)
*"The love that would hold me..."*

*Luis's lips move. He's trying to sing along, but only fragments come.*

**LUIS** (broken whisper)
*"...hold me..."*

**ADRIAN** (singing, louder now)
*"I promised care for thee..."*

*The room is blinding. Heat presses from all sides. Adrian doesn't stop.*

**[GAMEPLAY: A stillness mechanic, but inverted—the player must keep going, keep pressing forward, despite the visual noise telling them to stop.]**

**ADRIAN** (singing)
*"I've told you I'm sorry..."*

*Luis's body is almost transparent. Light streams through him. But his eyes—his eyes are fixed on Adrian.*

**LUIS** (barely audible)
*"...sorry..."*

**ADRIAN** (voice breaking, but continuing)
*"I was just too careleeeessss..."*

*He holds the note. The room vibrates with it.*

*And then—*

*Silence. For one moment, perfect silence.*

*Luis speaks. His own voice. Clear.*

**LUIS**
Adrian.

*Adrian stops singing. Stares.*

**LUIS**
I know who I am. I'm Luis. I'm your brother. I love you.

*Adrian sobs. Holds him tighter.*

**ADRIAN**
I love you too.

**LUIS**
(peaceful)
I'm scared. But I'm ready.

*The Light surges—*

---

## PHASE 4: THE LIGHT'S ATTEMPT

**Setting:** The room transformed. Reality bending. The Light fully present.

**Gameplay:** None. This is witnessed.

---

*The Light is everywhere now. It wraps around Luis, trying to complete the translation.*

*But something is wrong.*

*The Light pauses. Confused.*

*It cannot separate Luis from Adrian. The shapes are too connected. The pattern too intertwined.*

**THE LIGHT** (manifesting through Luis's voice, but hollow)
*Two. Not one. Pattern repeats. Cannot isolate.*

*It tries to take only Luis. Adrian holds tighter.*

**ADRIAN**
(defiant)
You can't have him alone. We're one pattern. We've always been one.

*The Light doesn't understand. But it recognizes something.*

*It begins to echo the song—trying to speak in the language Adrian used.*

**THE LIGHT** (through the air, through Luis, everywhere)
*"My... story. My... history..."*

*But it's wrong. The timing stretched. Notes colliding. An attempt at communication, failed.*

**ADRIAN**
(still holding Luis)
No. Like this.

*He sings again, softly now.*

**ADRIAN** (singing)
*"My story, my history..."*

*The Light listens. Tries again.*

**THE LIGHT**
*"My... story... my history..."*

*Closer. Still wrong, but closer.*

**LUIS** (faint, fading)
It's learning.

**ADRIAN**
(to the Light)
You want to understand? This is understanding. Holding someone. Caring for them. Even when it hurts.

*The Light pulses—confused, trying to process.*

**THE LIGHT**
*Hurt? Warmth is... hurt?*

**ADRIAN**
Sometimes. Yes.

*A pause. The Light considers.*

*Then, gently—so gently—it takes them both.*

---

## PHASE 5: THE END

**Setting:** Abstract. The brothers, entwined in light.

**Gameplay:** None.

---

*They are beyond the room now. Beyond the world, maybe. Everything is warmth and light.*

*Luis's form is fading. Translating. Becoming something else.*

*But Adrian holds him still.*

**LUIS**
(last words, his own)
I'm glad it was you. With me.

**ADRIAN**
(crying)
Four minutes behind you.

**LUIS**
(smile in his voice)
Eternal follower.

*Luis is gone. Translated. Absorbed.*

*But Adrian doesn't let go.*

**ADRIAN**
(to the Light)
I'm coming too.

*The Light hesitates. It didn't expect this. It doesn't understand why the other shape won't leave.*

**ADRIAN**
I'm not leaving him. I'm never leaving him.

*The Light does the only thing it knows.*

*It takes Adrian too.*

*The warmth becomes overwhelming. Adrian feels himself dissolving. But he also feels Luis—fragments of Luis, scattered within the Light—recognizing him.*

*The last thing he sees:*

*Luis's face, made of light, smiling.*

*The last thing he feels:*

*Not alone.*

*[SCREEN: White. Pure, gentle, endless white.]*

*[AUDIO: The song, one more time. But now it's the Light singing it. And this time—this time—it's almost right.]*

---

## PHASE 6: SILENCE

**Setting:** The room. Empty now. Dawn light through the windows.

**Gameplay:** None.

---

*The Light is gone. The brothers are gone.*

*The room is quiet. Dust settles. The covered windows let in pale, ordinary light.*

*In the center of the floor: a scorch mark. Two shapes, intertwined.*

*Outside, the convoy is gone.*

*But on a wall, written in ash, someone left a message:*

*"They didn't burn. They stayed."*

*[FADE TO BLACK]*

*[TEXT ON SCREEN:]*

*"The Light moved on. Still lonely. Still searching."*

*"But it carries fragments now—memories of warmth, of care, of two shapes that refused to separate."*

*"Somewhere, in the fractures of the sky, a song is playing."*

*"It's almost right."*

*[FINAL IMAGE: The sky. Fractures still present. But softer now. The hum, barely audible, sounds almost like humming.]*

*[CREDITS BEGIN]*

## VARIANT ENDINGS

### Low Shape Integrity Path

*If Luis's memories were not well-maintained:*

- Luis does not recognize Adrian at the end
- The song provides no protection
- The Light takes Luis completely, erasing his identity
- Adrian dies still holding someone who no longer knows him
- The ending emphasizes the failure to preserve what mattered

**Final Image:** The Light moves on, unchanged. It learned nothing. Luis is just another departure it doesn't understand.

---

### Exile Path

*If the brothers were separated from the convoy:*

- The final scene happens alone, in the wilderness
- No one witnesses; no one remembers
- The emotional beats remain, but there's no continuation
- Mae never knows what happened

**Final Image:** The convoy continues, unaware. The brothers become another forgotten loss.

---

### High Engagement Path

*If all systems were maintained optimally:*

- Luis remains fully lucid until the final moment
- He and Adrian sing together
- The Light clearly hesitates, clearly learns something
- Rhea, in the epilogue, hears Luis's song perfectly

**Final Image:** The fractures in the sky begin to retreat, slowly. The Light is grieving—but also growing.

## AUDIO DESIGN FOR THE ENDING

### The Song

*"My story, my history*
*I held the hope of being free*
*The love that would hold me*
*I promised care for thee*
*I've told you I'm sorry*
*I was just too careleeeessss..."*

**Performance Notes:**
- Adrian's version: Raw, breaking, desperate love
- Luis's fragments: Whispered, fading, reaching
- The Light's version: Stretched, echoed, almost right but not quite
- Final credits version: Pure, simple, finally correct

### Sound Design

- The hum should build throughout, becoming almost musical
- At the moment of translation, all sound should drop to silence
- The return of sound should be the song, transformed
- Credits should end with the hum, now peaceful

## CINEMATOGRAPHY NOTES

- Camera should remain grounded, intimate, never cutting away
- Close-ups on faces, hands, the space between
- The Light should fill the frame gradually, not suddenly
- The final embrace should be held—long, uncomfortable, necessary
- Fade to white should be slow, almost imperceptible

*"This is not an ending about winning. It's an ending about staying. About loving someone enough to go with them, even into the light."*

## AFTERMATH: THE WORLD AFTER

The Light's behavior changes after absorbing the brothers:

1. **It hesitates** — Where before it reached eagerly, now it pauses
2. **It echoes differently** — The mimicry has something new—tenderness
3. **It still doesn't understand** — But it *knows* there's something to understand
4. **It carries them** — Fragments of Luis and Adrian persist, scattered in the light

The world is not saved. The Light is not defeated. But something shifted.

The Light learned that touching can hurt.
The Light learned that some shapes are connected.
The Light learned a song.

And somewhere, in a convoy moving through the broken world, a child hears it singing.

---

*"They didn't save us. They just showed the sky what love looks like. Maybe that's enough. Maybe it has to be."*
— Mae Delacroix, final journal entry
