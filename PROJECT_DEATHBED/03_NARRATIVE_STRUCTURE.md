# PROJECT DEATHBED — NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

---

## OVERVIEW

**Total Runtime:** Approximately 8-12 hours of gameplay  
**Structure:** Three Acts with a Prologue and Epilogue  
**Perspective:** Primarily Adrian, with key Luis sequences  
**Pacing:** Slow burn with escalating emotional intensity  

---

## PROLOGUE: BEFORE THE BURNING

*"We didn't know what the sky was becoming. We just knew it was beautiful."*

### Setting
Eighteen months before main story. The brothers' apartment, the night of Luis's exposure.

### Purpose
- Establish the brothers' relationship before tragedy
- Show Luis whole, vibrant, *himself*
- Introduce the Light's first contact
- Create emotional baseline for all that follows

### Key Beats

1. **Evening Routine** — Luis practicing songs for his students; Adrian cooking dinner
2. **The Fracture** — First visible Light fracture outside their window; both mesmerized
3. **Luis's Choice** — He goes to the roof to see better; Adrian follows seconds too late
4. **The Touch** — Luis caught in descending Light; Adrian watches helplessly
5. **Aftermath** — Luis survives but changed; Adrian begins his vigil

### Emotional Core
Wonder becoming horror. The beautiful becoming lethal. The moment everything changed.

---

## ACT ONE: CARRYING

*"Some days he's almost himself. Those are the hardest days."*

**Duration:** ~3 hours  
**Primary Setting:** The convoy, in transit  
**Emotional Register:** Quiet dread, tender routine, fragile hope  

---

### Chapter 1: The Routine

**Setting:** Convoy vehicles, dawn transit

**Adrian's Day:**
- Morning symptom check on Luis
- Preparing medication and hydration
- Brief respite as Luis has a good morning
- Navigating convoy politics around a "touched" member

**Key Scenes:**
- *Symptom Journal* — Adrian documents Luis's condition, voiceover revealing medical reality
- *Morning Song* — Luis hums something from before; Adrian almost cries
- *Mae's Warning* — She reminds Adrian that sentimentality gets people killed

**Gameplay Focus:** Establishing caretaker mechanics; player learns the rhythm of keeping Luis stable.

---

### Chapter 2: The Convoy

**Setting:** Temporary camp, covered shelters

**The Community:**
- Meeting Mae, Tanner, Rhea, and supporting convoy members
- Understanding the social dynamics around the brothers
- Tensions between protection and fear

**Key Scenes:**
- *Tanner's Workshop* — Warmth, humor, normalcy; Tanner and Luis talk about music
- *Rhea's Drawing* — The child shows Adrian a sketch of something only she can see
- *Nightwatch* — Adrian on lookout duty; Mae joins him; they discuss what the Light might be

**Gameplay Focus:** Relationship building; navigating dialogue with emotional stakes.

---

### Chapter 3: The Good Day

**Setting:** A safe zone—a lakeside briefly free of fractures

**Brief Respite:**
- The convoy rests; guards lowered slightly
- Luis has extended lucidity; the brothers share a real conversation
- A moment that feels almost like before

**Key Scenes:**
- *By the Water* — Luis and Adrian talk about the future, knowing there isn't one
- *Luis's Request* — "When it happens, don't let me forget who I was."
- *The Hum* — Distant Light activity; the moment ends; transit resumes

**Gameplay Focus:** Memory preservation mechanics introduced; the player begins collecting "anchors" for Luis.

---

### Chapter 4: First Descent

**Setting:** Open road, Light activity increasing

**Crisis:**
- Fracture bloom during transit
- Convoy caught in the open
- Tanner exposed while saving a child
- Luis experiences violent vision episode

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Bloom* — Visual spectacle of Light fractures splitting across the sky
- *Tanner's Choice* — He shields a child; the Light touches him
- *Luis's Vision* — He sees fragments of the Light's loneliness; player experiences this
- *Aftermath* — Two convoy members dead; Tanner hiding symptoms; tension rising

**Gameplay Focus:** Light evasion mechanics; managing Luis during vision episodes.

**Act One Closing Image:** Adrian holding Luis as he sleeps, watching the sky through a crack in the covering, wondering how much time they have left.

---

## ACT TWO: BURNING

*"The Light doesn't understand what it's doing to us. And we can't explain."*

**Duration:** ~4 hours  
**Primary Setting:** Increasingly dangerous territory  
**Emotional Register:** Accelerating dread, desperate connection, fracturing hope  

---

### Chapter 5: The Deterioration

**Setting:** Convoy moving through urban ruins

**Luis Worsens:**
- Memory gaps becoming more frequent
- Speaking in layered voices
- Luminescent veins more visible
- Periods of being "elsewhere"

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Wrong Song* — Luis sings but the melody isn't his; he doesn't notice
- *Adrian's Fear* — Private moment of Adrian breaking down, alone
- *Tanner's Confession* — He admits his exposure to Luis; they share this burden

**Gameplay Focus:** Symptom management becomes harder; player must choose between stability and lucidity.

---

### Chapter 6: Rhea's Warning

**Setting:** Nighttime camp, Light activity high above

**The Child Speaks:**
- Rhea approaches Adrian with clarity unusual for her age
- She describes what she hears from the Light
- She warns that the Light is "almost ready" for Luis

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Listening* — Rhea translates the Light's signals; it's trying to "finish" something
- *Adrian's Question* — "What does it want with him?" Rhea's answer: "It wants to *be* him."
- *Luis Overhears* — He understands now; his identity is what the Light is trying to take

**Gameplay Focus:** Light communication puzzles; attempting to interpret the Light's signals.

---

### Chapter 7: Tanner's Departure

**Setting:** Convoy shelter during Light storm

**Loss:**
- Tanner's symptoms accelerate rapidly
- He asks Luis to sit with him
- He dies asking the convoy to remember him laughing
- The Light mimics his voice afterward

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Vigil* — Luis and Adrian sit with Tanner; the caretakers become the comforted
- *Tanner's Last Words* — Directed at Luis: "Outlast me, okay? Prove we can."
- *The Echo* — After Tanner dies, the Light repeats his laugh; it sounds almost right

**Gameplay Focus:** Ritual mechanics for honoring the dead; protecting the body from Light exposure.

---

### Chapter 8: The Shape

**Setting:** A brief stop at an abandoned hospital

**Adrian's Understanding:**
- Adrian searches for any medical intervention
- Finds nothing that can help
- Has conversation with Mae about what he truly understands

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Archive* — Adrian reads research notes from dead scientists; confirms the Light is learning
- *Mae's Confession* — She reveals her own brief exposure; she hears the hum sometimes
- *Adrian's Thesis* — He articulates it fully: the Light wants Luis's "shape"—his identity

**Gameplay Focus:** Environmental storytelling; piecing together the Light's history through found documents.

---

### Chapter 9: The Conversation

**Setting:** Luis during a vision episode; playable sequence

**Perspective Shift:**
- Player experiences the Light's communication attempt from Luis's perspective
- Fragments of memory, impossible geometries, desperate loneliness
- The Light trying to explain itself

**Key Scenes:**
- *Inside the Light* — Surreal sequence showing the Light's perception
- *The Question* — The Light asks, in its way, why Luis keeps leaving
- *Luis's Answer* — He tries to explain pain; the Light doesn't understand

**Gameplay Focus:** Non-verbal communication puzzle; the player must "speak" using the Light's logic.

**Act Two Closing Image:** Luis's eyes glowing faintly in the dark; Adrian watching, realizing they're almost out of time.

---

## ACT THREE: THE LAST SHAPE

*"I promised to take care of you. I never promised to let you go."*

**Duration:** ~3 hours  
**Primary Setting:** The convoy's final destination—and the end  
**Emotional Register:** Grief, defiance, love beyond death  

---

### Chapter 10: The Threshold

**Setting:** Convoy reaches a supposed sanctuary; finds it empty

**The End Approaches:**
- Luis's lucid moments become rare
- He speaks more in the Light's layered voice
- He asks Adrian to help him remember

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Empty Promise* — The sanctuary is abandoned; hope dies
- *Luis's Plea* — "Tell me who I am. Before I forget."
- *Memory Session* — Adrian recounts their childhood; Luis clings to each word

**Gameplay Focus:** Memory anchor mechanics critical; player must choose which memories to reinforce.

---

### Chapter 11: The Song

**Setting:** Night before the end

**Preparation:**
- Adrian knows tomorrow will be the last day
- He prepares himself emotionally
- The brothers share their final real conversation

**Key Scenes:**
- *The Request* — Luis asks Adrian to sing their song when it happens
- *The Promise* — Adrian agrees; he will not let Luis burn alone
- *Rhea's Gift* — The child gives Adrian a drawing of the brothers; she already knows

**Gameplay Focus:** Dialogue choices with profound weight; this conversation shapes the ending's details.

---

### Chapter 12: Departure

**Setting:** Dawn; Light activity reaching unprecedented levels

**The Climax:**
- Light descends on the convoy
- Luis begins final "translation"
- Adrian makes his choice

## EPILOGUE: WHAT REMAINS

*"The Light learned something from them. We just don't know what."*

### Setting
Days later. Mae leads the remaining convoy onward.

### Purpose
- Show life continuing despite loss
- Demonstrate the Light has changed, however slightly
- Give Rhea a moment of closure
- End on grief, hope, and continuation

### Key Beats

1. **Mae's Journal** — She writes about the brothers; the story survives
2. **The Light's Change** — Fractures seem to hesitate now; the hum sounds different
3. **Rhea's Vision** — She hears fragments of Luis's song, echoed correctly for the first time
4. **Continuation** — The convoy moves on; the world remains

### Emotional Core
Grief is not erasure. Love leaves marks. Even a lonely god can learn.
